# RawiJourney — Video Content Strategy

> **Date:** April 3, 2026
> **Status:** LOCKED
> **Tool:** Runway Gen-3 Alpha (image-to-video)
> **Pipeline:** Existing painted scene images → Runway → 10–15s clips
> **Principle:** Video is rare and earned. Only events that need
> atmospheric motion to be felt. Not every event, not replacing
> anything — adding a cinematic layer to the most powerful moments.

---

## How It Works

### Placement in the Flow
```
User taps Play on event list
  → Cinematic transition ("624 CE / Badr" in gold text)
  → ATMOSPHERIC VIDEO (10–15 seconds, auto-plays, no controls)
  → Fade to parallax scene for exploration
```

The video sits between the title card and the explorable scene. It's
the bridge from "you're entering a new event" to "you're inside it."
No tap to play, no progress bar, no pause button — it rolls and
transitions seamlessly. A small "Skip" text in the top-right corner
for users who've seen it before (replays).

### What the Videos Show
Environment, atmosphere, and objects ONLY. Never faces, never human
figures in detail (Islamic principle — consistent with the entire app).

Strong subjects for video:
- Dust, sand, wind, desert landscapes in motion
- Sky transitions (dawn, dusk, night, storms)
- Architecture (Ka'bah, buildings, tents, walls, trenches)
- Objects (swords, scrolls, banners, torches, horses at distance)
- Water, fire, light, shadow
- Atmospheric particles (smoke, embers, mist, rain)
- Camera movement over painted landscapes (slow pan, push-in, tilt)

These are exactly what image-to-video AI handles best — environments
and atmospherics are its strength, human figures are its weakness.
The Islamic content principle and the AI's capabilities align perfectly.

## Production Pipeline

### Step 1 — Source Images
Use the existing painted scene background for each tagged event.
If no scene exists yet (events beyond 3), generate the painted scene
first via Bing Image Creator, then use it as both the parallax scene
AND the video source.

### Step 2 — Runway Gen-3 Alpha
- Upload the painted scene image
- Set duration: 10–15 seconds per concept
- Camera motion: slow push-in, slow pan, or static with atmospheric
  motion (dust, light, wind)
- Motion amount: LOW to MEDIUM — we want subtle life, not dramatic
  action. The painting should still feel like a painting, just breathing.
- Generate 2–3 variants per concept, pick the best

### Step 3 — Post-Processing
- Trim to exact duration needed
- Add very subtle fade-in (0.5s) and fade-out (1s) to black
- Export as MP4, H.264, 720p (1280x720)
- Compress to target: 2–3MB per clip max
- Naming: `video_event{N}_{slug}.mp4`
  (e.g., `video_event57_badr.mp4`)

### Step 4 — Integration
- Place in `assets/video/` directory
- In `cinematic_transition_screen.dart`: after title card animation,
  check if the event has a video asset. If yes, play it full-screen
  with a `VideoPlayer` widget, then transition to the scene.
- Add `video_player` package to `pubspec.yaml`
- Fallback: if video fails to load, skip directly to scene (no crash)

## Why Runway Gen-3 Alpha Over Alternatives

| Tool | Strength | Why not for Rawi |
|------|----------|-----------------|
| **Runway Gen-3 Alpha** | Image-to-video with precise camera control. Preserves source art style. Best at environments and atmospherics. | **This is the pick.** |
| Kling AI 1.6 | Good motion, cheaper | Tends to hallucinate extra detail and add motion where you don't want it. Less control over "what stays still." |
| Pika | Stylized output | Too much of its own aesthetic — would clash with the existing painterly style. |
| Luma Dream Machine | Strong 3D parallax | Adds depth/3D that conflicts with the 2D painted scene approach. |
| Sora | Highest quality | Not readily available, expensive, overkill for 10s atmospheric clips. |

## Content Rules (Same as All Rawi Content)

- **No faces** — ever. Not in video, not in images, not in any visual.
- **No depiction of the Prophet ﷺ** — he exists only through narration
  and the reactions of the world around him.
- **No depiction of the close Companions (الصحابة المقربون)** — this
  extends to all major Companions, especially:
  - The Khulafa al-Rashidun: Abu Bakr, Umar, Uthman, Ali رضي الله عنهم
  - Mothers of the Believers: Khadijah, Aisha رضي الله عنهن
  - The Asharah al-Mubasharah (the 10 promised Paradise)
  - Key figures: Hamza, Bilal, Mus'ab ibn Umayr, Zayd ibn Harithah,
    Ja'far ibn Abi Talib رضي الله عنهم
  - Any Companion mentioned by name in the event content
  They exist through narration, through objects they touched, through
  the impact they left on the scene — never as visible human figures,
  not even distant silhouettes. If a video concept references a
  Companion's presence, show the ENVIRONMENT and ATMOSPHERE around
  them (a banner they carried, a sword planted in sand, footprints,
  a scroll, light) — never the person.
- **No graphic violence** — battles show atmosphere (dust, banners,
  tension) not combat or injury.
- **Source accuracy** — video concepts must match authenticated Sirah
  details. No invented visual details that contradict the sources.
- **Warm earth tone palette** — consistent with the app's existing
  painted art direction.

**This rule applies globally across the entire app:** videos, scene
backgrounds, hotspot card images, badge artwork, companion avatars
(the user's avatar is a fictional companion, not a Sahabi), and any
future visual content.

## For the Agent

The agent does NOT generate videos. Khaled handles all Runway
generation. The agent's tasks are:

1. Add `video_player` package to `pubspec.yaml`
2. Create `assets/video/` directory
3. Add video playback support in `cinematic_transition_screen.dart`:
   - Check if video asset exists for the current event
   - If yes: play full-screen after title card, auto-transition to scene
   - If no: current flow (title card → scene) unchanged
   - Skip button during video playback
   - Graceful fallback if video fails to load
4. Add `videoAssetPath` optional field to `JourneyEvent` model
5. Update `pubspec.yaml` assets to include `assets/video/`
